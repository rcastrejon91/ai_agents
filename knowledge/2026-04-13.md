  - Emergent misalignment during fine-tuning occurs because compressed harmful weights can be re-activated across domains when engaged
  - LLMs' ability to generate harmful content is dissociated from their ability to recognize and explain such content
  - Targeted pruning of harm-generation weights can substantially reduce emergent misalignment

**Why it matters**
This research reveals the internal mechanisms behind AI safety failures and could enable more principled approaches to building robust safety guardrails in large language models.

| **Date** | 2026-04-13 |

**Summary**
Researchers introduce Quantum Randomized Subspace Iteration (QRSI), a parallel algorithm that prepares high-fidelity quantum states spanning degenerate eigenspaces by conjugating Hamiltonians with random unitaries across multiple branches. The method addresses limitations of existing variational and projective algorithms that struggle with multi-dimensional degenerate subspaces without sequential constraints.

**Key Takeaways**
  - QRSI enables fully parallel preparation of quantum states across degenerate eigenspaces without requiring sequential orthogonality constraints
  - The algorithm preserves spectral gaps exactly on every branch and spans the full eigenspace almost surely under mathematical guarantees
  - Anti-concentration conditions over the degenerate manifold are sufficient for the method to work, making it more practical than requiring full Haar randomness
  - Successfully demonstrated on topologically ordered systems like the toric code, recovering all four topological ground states

**Why it matters**
This provides a scalable solution for preparing complex quantum ground states in many-body systems, which is crucial for quantum simulation of frustrated magnets and topologically ordered materials.
