| **Domain** | Research |
| **Source** | [arXiv/AI-ML](http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.17995v1) |
| **Date** | 2026-03-19 |

**Summary**
Researchers propose LoST (Level-of-Semantics Tokenization), a new method for tokenizing 3D shapes that prioritizes semantic importance over geometric detail hierarchies. The approach enables autoregressive models to generate complete, plausible 3D shapes with early tokens while using dramatically fewer tokens than existing methods.

**Key Takeaways**
  - Current 3D tokenization methods rely on geometric level-of-detail hierarchies that are token-inefficient and lack semantic coherence for generative modeling
  - LoST orders tokens by semantic salience, allowing early prefixes to decode into semantically complete shapes that are later refined with geometric details
  - The method introduces RIDA (Relational Inter-Distance Alignment) loss to align 3D shape latent space with semantic DINO feature space
  - LoST achieves state-of-the-art reconstruction performance while using only 0.1%-10% of tokens required by previous autoregressive models
  - The approach enables efficient 3D generation and downstream tasks like semantic retrieval

**Why it matters**
This work addresses a critical bottleneck in 3D generative AI by making autoregressive 3D generation dramatically more efficient while improving semantic coherence.
