### 13. 🔬 Polynomial Speedup in Diffusion Models with the Multilevel Euler-Maruyama Method

| **Domain** | Research |
| **Source** | [arXiv/AI-ML](http://arxiv.org/abs/2603.24594v1) |
| **Date** | 2026-03-26 |

**Summary**
This paper introduces the Multilevel Euler-Maruyama (ML-EM) method for solving stochastic differential equations (SDEs) and ordinary differential equations (ODEs) using multiple approximators of increasing accuracy and computational cost. The method achieves polynomial speedups by using many evaluations of cheaper approximators and few evaluations of expensive ones, particularly beneficial for diffusion models where different UNet sizes serve as the approximators.

**Key Takeaways**
  - ML-EM improves computational complexity from ε^(-γ-1) to ε^(-γ) for drifts in the 'Harder than Monte Carlo' regime where γ>2
  - The method allows solving SDEs at the same computational cost as a single evaluation of the most accurate drift approximator
  - In diffusion models, different UNet sizes serve as the multiple approximation levels, enabling sampling equivalent to one evaluation of the largest UNet
  - Experimental results show up to 4x speedups on CelebA 64x64 image generation with γ≈2.5
  - The polynomial nature of the speedup suggests even greater improvements for larger networks used in practical applications

**Why it matters**
This work addresses the computational bottleneck in diffusion model sampling by providing a theoretically grounded method to achieve polynomial speedups, which could significantly reduce the cost of large-scale generative AI applications.
