**Summary**
This research proposes a new equilibrium concept that minimizes coalitional deviation incentives rather than eliminating them entirely, addressing the problem that traditional multi-player equilibria often don't exist. The authors develop computational algorithms for finding equilibria that minimize average or maximum gains from coordinated deviations by coalitions of players.

**Key Takeaways**

  - The framework enables solving the Exploitability Welfare Frontier problem, balancing social welfare against vulnerability to exploitation

This work addresses a fundamental gap in game theory by providing computationally feasible equilibrium concepts that account for coordinated strategic behavior, which is crucial for designing robust multi-agent systems and markets.
