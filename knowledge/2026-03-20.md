**Summary**
Researchers introduce measurement-induced quantum neural networks (MINNs), where quantum circuit measurements dynamically determine subsequent entangling gates, creating adaptive architectures with history-dependent dynamics. **Key Takeaways**

  - Matchgate MINNs allow exact fermionic simulation while maintaining trainability through gradient estimators
  - The approach shows effective performance across diverse applications including continuous optimization, image classification, and ground-state search
  - Generic MINNs are expected to be classically intractable, representing a potential quantum advantage

**Why it matters**
