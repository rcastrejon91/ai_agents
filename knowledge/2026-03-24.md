**Summary**
UNITE introduces an end-to-end training approach for latent diffusion models that jointly optimizes image tokenization and generation in a single stage, eliminating the need for complex staged training. The method uses a shared Generative Encoder that performs both tokenization (from images) and generation (from noise) as unified latent inference tasks under different conditioning regimes.

**Key Takeaways**
  - Traditional latent diffusion models require complex two-stage training where tokenizers must be trained first before the diffusion model can use the frozen latent space
  - UNITE's key insight is that tokenization and generation are fundamentally the same latent inference problem with different conditioning inputs
  - The unified architecture achieves near state-of-the-art performance (FID 2.12 and 1.73) without adversarial losses or pretrained encoders like DINO
  - Joint training enables gradients to shape a shared latent space, creating a 'common latent language' between tokenization and generation tasks
  - The approach demonstrates feasibility across both image and molecule modalities

**Why it matters**
This work simplifies the training pipeline for latent diffusion models by eliminating staged training requirements, potentially making high-quality generative models more accessible and efficient to develop.
